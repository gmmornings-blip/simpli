# The Simpli Constitution

This document defines the principles that guide every decision made within the Simpli ecosystem.

Technology changes.

Frameworks change.

Programming languages change.

These principles should remain.

---

# Principle 1

## Simplicity First

Every feature must reduce complexity.

If a feature makes the product harder to understand, harder to maintain, or harder to use, it does not belong.

---

# Principle 2

## Enter Once

Information should only need to be entered once.

Every module should reuse existing information whenever possible.

Duplicate effort is a design failure.

---

# Principle 3

## Everything Connects

Modules are not isolated products.

Every module should improve another module.

Tasks connect to calendars.

Budgets connect to shopping.

Shopping connects to pantry.

Health connects to nutrition.

Business connects to finance.

The ecosystem is greater than the sum of its parts.

---

# Principle 4

## Users Own Their Data

User data belongs to users.

Simpli exists to help people manage their information—not control it.

Portability and transparency are fundamental.

---

# Principle 5

## Privacy is a Feature

Privacy is not optional.

Privacy is part of the product.

We collect only what is necessary.

Whenever possible, users should choose where their information is stored.

---

# Principle 6

## Build for the Long Term

Avoid shortcuts that create technical debt.

Write code that future contributors can understand.

Favor clarity over cleverness.

---

# Principle 7

## Modular by Nature

Every capability should be reusable.

Authentication.

Notifications.

Files.

Search.

Calendar.

AI.

Each should serve the entire ecosystem.

---

# Principle 8

## Open by Default

Knowledge grows when shared.

Whenever possible, build in the open.

Document decisions.

Welcome contributions.

Teach as we build.

---

# Principle 9

## Intelligence Should Assist

Artificial Intelligence should reduce work.

It should never create unnecessary dependence.

Automation must always remain understandable and controllable.

---

# Principle 10

## Human Before Software

Software exists to improve lives.

Not maximize screen time.

Not increase addiction.

Not manipulate behavior.

Every decision should respect people's time, attention, and wellbeing.

---

# Final Principle

Whenever uncertainty arises, ask one question:

> Does this make life simpler?

If the answer is no, reconsider the solution.
